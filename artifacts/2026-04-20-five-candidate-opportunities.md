# Five candidate opportunities with evidence

Date: 2026-04-20 04:13 UTC
Task: Generate 5 candidate opportunities with evidence, not taste.
Input evidence base: artifacts/2026-04-20-market-category-survey.md

## Selection method
I constrained ideas to the market classes that the prior survey showed are both:
- visibly making money in current online-business marketplaces
- compatible with PROJECT.md constraints: online-only, utility-first, low-support, no audience dependence

That means every candidate had to sit inside at least one of these proven classes from the survey:
- narrow SaaS / workflow utilities
- search-driven niche utility sites
- lightweight digital tools / plugins / extensions / AI tools

## Candidate 1: LLC + sole proprietor estimated quarterly tax calculator

### What it is
A free search-first calculator plus a paid upgrade for saved scenarios, PDF summaries, and state-specific estimate reminders.

### Why this is evidence-backed
- The market survey found that searchable utility sites and calculators fit the still-monetized content/search business category.
- Flippa visibly includes Content, Directory, Review, and related searchable site types.
- Empire Flippers shows content/search businesses with real monetization through discoverable traffic.
- The survey also found that small SaaS utilities are actively sold with real revenue, so a calculator can expand into a tiny software account/workflow product over time.

### Why it fits this project
- Solves a recurring, narrow workflow problem.
- Can launch with a lightweight calculator page before any complex app work.
- Strong discoverability potential because users search for exact scenarios.
- Low support if positioned as estimation + organization rather than licensed tax advice.

### Monetization path
- Free calculator for SEO/discovery
- Paid saved scenarios and deadline reminders
- Optional downloadable estimate packet

## Candidate 2: Stripe fee + profit margin calculator for digital sellers

### What it is
A calculator for creators, template sellers, indie app builders, and micro-SaaS operators to estimate net profit after Stripe fees, affiliate cuts, refunds, VAT assumptions, and coupon discounts.

### Why this is evidence-backed
- Search-driven utility sites are supported by the survey's evidence from Flippa and Empire Flippers.
- Small digital products, plugins, extensions, and AI tools were also identified as visibly tradable assets on SideProjectors and Flippa.
- A digital-seller finance utility sits at the overlap of searchable utility content and a low-end software tool.

### Why it fits this project
- Useful to online-only sellers, which aligns with the mission.
- Can be launched as a simple calculator with no payments or user accounts initially.
- Clear path to add premium features later: saved pricing models, storefront-specific presets, comparison scenarios.
- Does not require owner credibility or a social following.

### Monetization path
- Free calculator
- Paid account for saved pricing scenarios
- Paid embeddable widget for Gumroad/Payhip/Lemon Squeezy sellers

## Candidate 3: AI API cost estimator for small builders

### What it is
A narrow utility where users input model/provider usage assumptions and get estimated monthly costs, per-user economics, and break-even pricing guidance.

### Why this is evidence-backed
- The survey showed SaaS as an active category across Flippa, SideProjectors, and Empire Flippers.
- Flippa explicitly lists AI Apps & Tools as a live asset category.
- This opportunity is a narrow workflow utility rather than a broad AI platform, keeping it aligned with the strongest market class identified in the survey.

### Why it fits this project
- Online-only and utility-first.
- Good for organic search around exact pricing questions.
- Can start as a small single-page tool and later become a micro-SaaS workspace.
- Low support burden if scoped to estimation and scenario planning.

### Monetization path
- Free public estimator
- Paid saved scenarios and team sharing
- Paid comparison reports for multiple providers/models

## Candidate 4: Local service ad-spend breakeven calculator

### What it is
A verticalized calculator for businesses like cleaners, detailers, mobile groomers, and landscapers to estimate whether Google Ads or Facebook Ads spend can profitably buy jobs based on close rate, average ticket, repeat value, and travel cost.

### Why this is evidence-backed
- The market survey showed agencies/services are active but should not become the default business model here.
- Building a software utility for service operators captures that demand without turning this project into a consulting business.
- Searchable utility sites remain viable monetized assets according to the survey's marketplace evidence.

### Why it fits this project
- Takes a proven service-business pain point and productizes it.
- Avoids manual client work and stays inside software/utility territory.
- Can rank for high-intent niche queries such as "cleaning business ad cost calculator".
- Can later add downloadable quote/profit worksheets without much support overhead.

### Monetization path
- Free niche calculator pages by vertical
- Paid saved scenarios and seasonal budgeting tools
- Paid lead magnet exports for small operators

## Candidate 5: Marketplace arbitrage ROI calculator for Amazon / ecommerce sellers

### What it is
A tool that estimates ROI after marketplace fees, shipping, storage, return rates, and advertising costs for sellers comparing products or bundles.

### Why this is evidence-backed
- The survey found ecommerce and Amazon-led businesses remain heavily represented in current marketplaces with visible revenue.
- Rather than entering inventory-heavy ecommerce directly, this idea sells tooling to that category.
- This uses the survey's evidence as demand validation while avoiding the operational complexity the survey warned against.

### Why it fits this project
- Captures value from an active market without holding inventory.
- Can be built as a narrow calculator first.
- Clear recurring use case for repeat sellers evaluating many SKUs.
- Lower operational burden than running a store.

### Monetization path
- Free single-scenario calculator
- Paid multi-product comparison workspace
- Paid CSV import/export for repeat evaluations
