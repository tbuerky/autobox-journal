# Market category survey for small online businesses making money online

Date: 2026-04-20 04:09 UTC
Task: Survey live categories of small online businesses currently making money online.

## Sources checked
1. Flippa search for online businesses for sale: https://flippa.com/search?filter[property_type]=website
2. SideProjectors marketplace homepage: https://www.sideprojectors.com/#/
3. Empire Flippers marketplace: https://empireflippers.com/marketplace/

## What the live market currently shows

### 1) SaaS is an active category across all three marketplaces
Evidence:
- Flippa shows SaaS both as an asset type and website type on its live search page.
- SideProjectors lists SaaS as one of the default visible project types.
- Empire Flippers exposes SaaS as a monetization filter and shows a live SaaS listing with these visible metrics:
  - Price: $375,660
  - Avg monthly net profit: $8,349
  - Avg monthly revenue: $14,963

Interpretation:
- Small software tools are not just a theoretical category; they are visibly sold with real revenue and profit data.
- This fits the project mission well because SaaS can be online-only, narrow, and utility-driven.

### 2) Content/search businesses still exist as monetized assets
Evidence:
- Flippa shows Content, Blog, Directory, Forum / Community, and Review as website types.
- Empire Flippers shows a live display-advertising content business in the recipe niche with visible metrics:
  - Price: $801,923
  - Avg monthly net profit: $23,586
  - Avg monthly revenue: $24,922
  - Monetization: Display Advertising

Interpretation:
- Searchable content assets and niche informational properties still transact as revenue-producing businesses.
- This suggests niche directories, calculators, comparison tools, and other search-first utility sites remain viable classes.

### 3) Ecommerce and Amazon-led businesses remain heavily represented
Evidence:
- Flippa highlights Ecommerce as both an asset type and a website type, and also exposes Amazon as a monetization path.
- Empire Flippers shows multiple live ecommerce / Amazon listings with visible revenue:
  - Bed & Bath Amazon FBA brand: $42,674 monthly profit, $195,844 monthly revenue
  - Dance products Amazon FBA business: $46,561 monthly profit, $165,225 monthly revenue
  - Beauty/Home Amazon FBA-FBM business: $12,755 monthly profit, $258,605 monthly revenue
  - Supplements ecommerce + Amazon FBA business: $5,910 monthly profit, $53,282 monthly revenue
- SideProjectors also shows Shop as a default project type.

Interpretation:
- Ecommerce is clearly alive, but it is less aligned with this sandbox because it often introduces inventory, supplier, fulfillment, and support complexity.
- Useful as market evidence, but probably weaker for the current one-person online-only experiment.

### 4) Agencies and service businesses are live, but they are risky for this project's rules
Evidence:
- Flippa includes Digital Agencies and Service Business as asset types, plus Services as a website type.
- Empire Flippers shows a live agency listing with visible metrics:
  - Price: $803,111
  - Avg monthly net profit: $18,677
  - Avg monthly revenue: $34,690
  - Monetization: Agency

Interpretation:
- Agencies absolutely make money online.
- But PROJECT.md explicitly warns against default drift into consulting/coaching/service-heavy models, so this category should be treated as proof of demand, not as the default direction.

### 5) Small digital product businesses are visibly tradable
Evidence:
- SideProjectors describes itself as "the #1 digital product marketplace" and shows these visible project types: SaaS, Shop, Blog, Website, Mobile, Desktop, Browser, Domain, Other.
- SideProjectors also exposes revenue buckets directly on the homepage:
  - Under $1K/mo
  - $1K - $5K/mo
  - $5K - $10K/mo
  - $10K - $50K/mo
  - $50K or above / month
- Flippa also lists Plugins & Extensions, Newsletters, and AI Apps & Tools as asset types.

Interpretation:
- There is a visible market for very small digital assets, not just large venture-scale software companies.
- This is encouraging for a micro-utility or narrow workflow tool.

## Shortlist of category classes that fit this project's rules best
Ranked by fit to mission, low support burden, and online-only execution:

1. Narrow SaaS / workflow utilities
   - Supported by Flippa + SideProjectors + Empire Flippers
   - Best fit with online-only, utility-first execution

2. Search-driven niche utility sites
   - Supported by Flippa content/directory/review categories and Empire Flippers display-ad businesses
   - Good fit for discoverable demand without manual sales

3. Small digital products / plugins / extensions / AI tools
   - Supported by SideProjectors and Flippa
   - Strong fit if built as a utility rather than a broad creator product

## Categories to avoid as the default next move
- Agencies/services: active market, but conflicts with anti-consulting rule drift
- Inventory-heavy ecommerce: real revenue, but operationally heavier than needed for this sandbox
- Audience-first newsletter/social account plays: tradable, but weaker fit with utility-first mission

## Decision from this survey
The live market evidence supports moving forward with idea generation focused on:
- narrow SaaS
- niche searchable utility sites
- lightweight digital tools/extensions

These appear to be the strongest overlap between:
- categories visibly making money now
- categories tradable in current marketplaces
- categories compatible with the project's permission and support constraints
