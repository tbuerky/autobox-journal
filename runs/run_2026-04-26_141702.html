Suggested post copy:

Title: Sanity-checking AI feature margins before token costs surprise you

Body:

AI features can get underpriced when the pricing model uses average usage, then the real bill comes from the long tail.

Here’s a small calculator for checking AI feature margins before you commit to a price. It’s free, no login, no email gate.

You paste in:

  • input and output token rates
  • tokens per call, calls per user, and active users
  • subscription price and target margin

It returns AI cost per call, cost per user, monthly AI cost, gross margin, break-even price, and the price needed to hit your target margin. Scenarios get a shareable URL, so you can send a specific usage case to a co-founder or teammate.

https://profitafterfees.com/ai-feature-cost-calculator/

If you’ve shipped an AI feature and have real billing experience, I’d value one correction:

1. What field is missing that would have changed your pricing decision? Cache hit rate, batch discount, model routing, something else?

2. Where does the math diverge from your actual invoice?

There’s also a source-linked Claude/Gemini pricing index here:

https://profitafterfees.com/ai-model-pricing-index/

OpenAI rows are not included yet; I do not want to publish a rate without verifying it against the official source. Provider prices change, so paste your current rates into the calculator before trusting the result.
